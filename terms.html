Section 3 — How the Service Works

3.1 Database

Clir maintains its own Database containing information on a wide range of food products. This Database is built from:

  1. Contributor input: Users can directly add or modify food information through the App.
  2. Public information and manufacturer-supplied data: Based on publicly disclosed ingredient information shown on product labels.

The Publisher does not warrant the complete reliability of the food ingredient information contained in the Database. The information the App provides is a relay of the information shown on product labels; the Publisher does not directly analyze ingredients itself. Ingredient errors or recognition errors may occur.

3.2 Analysis Method

The App uses barcode scanning or OCR (optical character recognition) technology to gather food information and compares it against the Database to provide allergen presence and dietary restriction information.

Allergen analysis is based on the list of ingredients shown on the product label. However, the following situations may not fall within the scope of the App's analysis:

  • Cross-contamination at manufacturing facilities
  • Allergens listed only as warnings, such as "May contain..."
  • Ingredients not accurately listed on the label by the manufacturer

All Analysis Results provided by the App are reference opinions based on the Database and do not reflect the medical condition, allergic sensitivity, or specific health circumstances of any individual user. Expressions such as "Safe to consume," "Caution required," and "Do not consume" provided by the App are reference opinions based on Database information and are not medical determinations. The Publisher makes no warranty regarding the safety or health benefits of the products themselves.
